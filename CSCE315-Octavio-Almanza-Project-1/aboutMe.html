My Life

My name is Octavio Almanza, and I was born in Laredo, a small border city in south Texas between Mexico and the United States. I spent most of my childhood with my mother’s family in Mexico, which enabled me to adopt her culture, religion, and traditions. When I reached grade school, my mother decided to enroll me as a student in the US. Although this transition came with some personal hardships given the language and cultural barriers, my family knew it would be important for my future.

My introduction to computer science began with my 8th grade technology class. I was tasked with creating basic programs in Scratch↗, which is a block-based, visual programming website aimed at younger audiences. I found myself spending much of my free time working on small projects on my middle school’s rental laptop, which consisted of small platformer games and recreations of classic arcade games. I recall proudly showing my finished projects to my family and how fulfilling it felt to see them in action. Although Scratch is extremely user-friendly and limited in scope, it exposed me to the many challenges and possibilities that came with programming. This experience would lead me to enter my high school’s robotics team and computer science track.

My experience as a programmer for the FIRST Robotics Competition enabled me to work with Java, explore the basics of object-oriented design, and how computers interact with external devices such as motors and controllers. Working with high-level abstractions and devices furthered my curiosity about how everything worked under the hood, and it solidified my decision to pursue the field for my undergraduate studies.

As my high school graduation approached, I had a hard time deciding where I would continue my studies. Although I would have liked to stay in a community college near my family and friends, my parents convinced me to pursue any opportunities that arose. This is why upon receiving my acceptance letter to A&M, I decided to further my education away from home. My family has made many expenses and sacrifices to ensure I could follow my passions, and I will ensure nothing goes to waste by continuing to succeed in all my educational endeavors.
